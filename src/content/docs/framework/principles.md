# Four Principles

Every implementation of Programmable Assurance follows four behavioral principles.

These principles describe *how* governance behaves rather than *what* technology is used.

---

## 1. Intent Must Be Executable

Governance that exists only as documentation cannot influence decisions.

Intent must be represented in a form that systems can evaluate continuously.

---

## 2. Enforcement Must Be Continuous

Governance should operate whenever relevant decisions occur.

Continuous enforcement transforms audits from discovery events into confirmation events.

---

## 3. Every Decision Must Be Accountable

Every governance decision must identify:

- who made it
- why it was made
- what policy applied
- what evidence supports it

Anonymous governance is not defensible governance.

---

## 4. Outcomes Must Improve Intent

Governance that never learns eventually becomes wrong.

Observed outcomes should continuously refine future governance decisions.

This creates the Assurance Feedback Loop.
