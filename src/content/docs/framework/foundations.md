# Foundations

Every engineering discipline begins with assumptions.

Civil engineering assumes gravity.

Computer science assumes computation.

Zero Trust assumes trust is continuously verified.

Programmable Assurance assumes that governance should continuously align intent with outcomes.

Everything else follows from that assumption.

---

## Governance Exists To Influence Outcomes

Governance is not documentation.

Documentation records governance.

Governance exists to influence decisions.

If governance cannot influence a decision when it matters, it has failed regardless of how well it is documented.

---

## Governance Is A Living System

Organizations change continuously.

Technology changes continuously.

Threats change continuously.

Regulations change continuously.

Governance cannot remain static while everything around it evolves.

Programmable Assurance treats governance as a living system that continuously learns from operational reality.

---

## The Intent-Reality Gap

Every governance failure begins when organizational intent becomes disconnected from operational reality.

Policies may exist.

Controls may exist.

Audits may exist.

Yet the organization cannot demonstrate that intent and reality still agree.

Programmable Assurance exists to close that gap.
